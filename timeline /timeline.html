1930's

1931:Peter Lawford’s film debut was at age 8 in the British film Poor Old Bill
1983:Dean Martin began touring with Watkins

1940's

1940:>Dean Martin chnages his name from Dino Paul Crocetti to Dean Martin
1943:Dean Martin signed an exclusive contract with MCA to sing at the Riobamba Room in New York
1946:Dean Martin signed a contract and recorded four songs with Diamond Records
1947:Sammy Davis Jr’s career began to rise to new heights in 1947 when the trio opened for Frank Sinatra at the Capitol Theatre in New York.

1950's

1950:Joey Bishop started a friendship with Frank Sinatra, which resulted in Bishop’s inclusion in the Rat Pack
1954: Sammy Davis Jr. Signed with Decca Records
1955:Sammy Davis Jr. first two albums,“Starring Sammy Davis Jr.” and “Sammy Davis Jr. Sings Just for Lovers”, were released to both critical acclaim and commercial success.

1960's

1960:Rat Pack stars in Ocean’s Eleven
1961: Lawford and Sinatra had a falling out in 1961 over brother-in-law Robert Kennedy’s objections to Sinatra’s alleged Mafia connections
1962: Rat Pack Stars in Sergeants 3
1964:Rat Pack stars in Seven Hoods
1964: Dean Martin recorded his blockbuster hit, “Everybody Loves Somebody,”
1965:Frank Sinatra receives Achievement Award and headlined the 1965 Newport Jazz Festival with Count Basie’s Orchestra. This period also marked his Las Vegas debut
1966:Frank Sinatra had a big hit in 1966 with the Billboard No. 1 track “Strangers in the Night,” which won a Grammy for record of the year
1967:Frank Sinatra’s duet “Something Stupid” with daughter Nancy, No. 1 for four weeks with “Something Stupid” in spring 1967
